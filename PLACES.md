## 🥐 Food & Drink (20)
*Bakeries, bistros, markets, wine bars*

### Du Pain et des Idées
**Bakery** · Canal Saint-Martin, 10th arr. · € — pastries 2-7€ · ★ 4.3 (4,292 Google reviews)
📍 34 Rue Yves Toudic, 75010 Paris (48.8708, 2.3629)
> Widely considered one of the best artisanal bakeries in Paris, famous for its pistachio-chocolate 'escargot' pastry and pain des amis. Gorgeous 1875 storefront near Canal Saint-Martin; expect a line, and note it's closed weekends.
🗣️ Google reviewers: The pistachio escargot, sacristain and croissants are worth the hype at this gorgeous 1875-decor bakery; complaints cite long lines, brusque cashier service and weekend closures.
💡 *Go on a weekday before 11am for the freshest escargots — closed Saturday and Sunday.*

### Pierre Hermé
**Patisserie** · Saint-Germain-des-Prés, 6th arr. · €€ — macarons ~2.50€ each, pastries 8-12€ · ★ 4.4 (3,169 Google reviews)
📍 72 Rue Bonaparte, 75006 Paris (48.8513, 2.3327)
> The 'Picasso of pastry,' beloved for inventive macarons — the rose-lychee-raspberry Ispahan is iconic. Reviewers consistently rank his macarons above Ladurée's.
🗣️ Google reviewers: Exceptional macarons with inventive flavors like the Ispahan and helpful staff; complaints focus on high prices and occasionally dismissive service for small purchases.
💡 *Order the Ispahan macaron or full Ispahan pastry, and try the seasonal 'Fetish' flavors.*

### Stohrer
**Patisserie** · Montorgueil, 2nd arr. · € — pastries 3-8€ · ★ 4.4 (4,000 Google reviews)
📍 51 Rue Montorgueil, 75002 Paris (48.8653, 2.3469)
> Paris's oldest patisserie, founded 1730 by Louis XV's pastry chef and birthplace of the baba au rhum. Historic painted interior and old-school classics like puits d'amour.
🗣️ Google reviewers: Reviewers love the baba au rhum and classic pastries in the gorgeous 1730 interior; occasional gripes about tourist crowds, prices and curt counter service.
💡 *Order the original baba au rhum and eat it strolling pedestrian Rue Montorgueil.*

### Poilâne
**Bakery** · Saint-Germain-des-Prés, 6th arr. · € — punitions ~1€, miche slices, whole 1.9kg miche ~12€ · ★ 4.2 (1,119 Google reviews)
📍 8 Rue du Cherche-Midi, 75006 Paris (48.8516, 2.3289)
> Legendary sourdough bakery (since 1932) whose wood-fired miche is shipped worldwide. Loved for apple tarts and butter shortbread 'punitions'.
🗣️ Google reviewers: The legendary sourdough miche, punitions cookies and apple tarts are celebrated; a recurring complaint is cold or haughty service.
💡 *You don't need a whole miche — buy it by the quarter or in slices; punition cookies make perfect cheap souvenirs.*

### Angelina
**Dessert** · Tuileries/Louvre, 1st arr. · €€ — hot chocolate ~9-10€, pastries 10-15€ · ★ 4.2 (22,000 Google reviews)
📍 226 Rue de Rivoli, 75001 Paris (48.8652, 2.3286)
> Belle Époque tearoom (1903) famous for 'L'Africain' ultra-thick hot chocolate with whipped cream and the chestnut Mont-Blanc. Touristy with long lines, but most say the chocolat chaud is worth it.
🗣️ Google reviewers: Reviewers adore the famously thick L'Africain hot chocolate and Mont-Blanc in the Belle Époque salon; complaints cite long queues, high prices and rushed, touristy service.
💡 *Skip the queue by buying bottled hot chocolate from the takeaway counter, or come at 7:30am opening on weekdays.*

### Berthillon
**Ice Cream** · Île Saint-Louis, 4th arr. · € — scoops ~3-4€ · ★ 4.5 (4,407 Google reviews)
📍 29-31 Rue Saint-Louis en l'Île, 75004 Paris (48.8519, 2.3568)
> Paris's most famous glacier, family-run on Île Saint-Louis since 1954 — intense fruit sorbets and salted caramel. Expect queues; the flagship quirkily closes part of summer.
🗣️ Google reviewers: The intensely flavored sorbets and salted-caramel ice cream are called the gold standard in Paris, though some find scoops small and pricey with long waits.
💡 *If the shop is closed or the line is huge, nearby café terraces like Le Flore en l'Île serve the same Berthillon scoops.*

### L'As du Fallafel
**Street Food** · Le Marais, 4th arr. · € — falafel pita ~9-10€ · ★ 4.3 (14,000 Google reviews)
📍 34 Rue des Rosiers, 75004 Paris (48.8574, 2.3591)
> The legendary Marais falafel joint — overstuffed pitas of crispy falafel, fried eggplant, cabbage and harissa that reviewers call the best they've ever had. The line is constant but fast.
🗣️ Google reviewers: The generously stuffed falafel pita with eggplant and harissa is called the best in Paris and great value; complaints mention long lines, chaotic crowds and brusque, rushed service.
💡 *Use the takeaway window (much faster) and eat in nearby Place des Vosges. Closed Friday evening and Saturday.*

### Breizh Café Le Marais
**Crêperie** · Le Marais, 3rd arr. · €€ — galettes 12-18€, crêpes 8-12€ · ★ 4.2 (3,367 Google reviews)
📍 109 Rue Vieille du Temple, 75003 Paris (48.8606, 2.3633)

🗣️ Google reviewers: Authentic buckwheat galettes, Brittany ciders and friendly staff at fair prices; complaints mention waits without reservations and cramped seating.
💡 *Book ahead or go mid-afternoon; pair with Breton cider and finish with the salted-caramel butter crêpe.*

### Marché des Enfants Rouges
**Market** · Haut Marais, 3rd arr. · € — market plates and sandwiches 8-15€ · ★ 4.3 (13,000 Google reviews)
📍 39 Rue de Bretagne, 75003 Paris (48.8628, 2.3622)
> Paris's oldest covered market (1628) — produce, cheese and flower stalls plus counters serving Moroccan couscous, Japanese bentos, Italian and crêpes. Buzzy local vibe; weekend lunch is packed.
🗣️ Google reviewers: Reviewers love the lively, authentic atmosphere and variety of French, Moroccan, Lebanese, Italian and Japanese stalls, though it gets very crowded at lunch with limited shared seating.
💡 *Weekday before noon to snag a stool; the Moroccan stand and Chez Alain Miam Miam are the cult favorites. Closed Mondays.*

### Chez Alain Miam Miam

💡 *Come hungry and patient — one sandwich feeds two; line is shortest at opening. Closed Monday-Tuesday.*

### Bistrot Paul Bert

### Chez Janou

### Bouillon Chartier Grands Boulevards

### Café de Flore

## 🗼 Classic Touristy (18)
*The must-see landmarks, with 2026 prices and statuses*

### Eiffel Tower
**Monument** · 7th arr. · 36.70€ summit lift, 23.50€ 2nd-floor lift, 14.80€ stairs (adults) · ★ 4.7 (450,000 Google reviews)
📍 Champ de Mars, 5 Avenue Anatole France, 75007 Paris (48.8584, 2.2945)
> The undisputed icon of Paris with sweeping views from three levels. Long security lines and sold-out slots — book official timed tickets weeks ahead, especially the summit.
🗣️ Google reviewers: The night-time sparkle and observation-deck views are unforgettable; reviewers repeatedly warn about long security lines, dense crowds, and pickpockets around the base.
💡 *Take the stairs to the 2nd floor (cheaper, shorter queue) then buy the summit lift upgrade there.*

### Louvre Museum
**Museum** · 1st arr. · 32€ non-EU adults (2026), 22€ EU adults, free under 18 · ★ 4.7 (359,000 Google reviews)
📍 Rue de Rivoli, 75001 Paris (48.8606, 2.3376)
> The world's largest art museum, home to the Mona Lisa and Venus de Milo. A must, but serious crowds around the Mona Lisa; timed-entry reservation is effectively mandatory.
🗣️ Google reviewers: An incomparable, essential art collection; the recurring complaint is overcrowding around the Mona Lisa and the museum's exhausting size.
💡 *Skip the Pyramid queue via the Carrousel du Louvre (99 Rue de Rivoli) or Porte des Lions entrance; Wednesday/Friday evenings are thinnest.*

### Notre-Dame Cathedral
**Church** · Île de la Cité, 4th arr. · Free (cathedral); towers 16€, Treasury 12€ · ★ 4.7 (93,686 Google reviews)
📍 6 Parvis Notre-Dame - Place Jean-Paul II, 75004 Paris (48.853, 2.3499)
> Reopened December 2024 after the fire — the restored interior is breathtakingly bright, and the bell towers reopened September 2025. Free, but walk-in waits hit 2-3 hours in peak season.
🗣️ Google reviewers: Reviewers rave about the breathtaking post-fire restoration, stained glass, and free entry; the main complaint is very long entrance queues since reopening.
💡 *Book a free timed slot on the official app (opens 2-3 days ahead) or arrive before 8am; tower tickets sell out fast.*

### Basilique du Sacré-Cœur
**Church** · Montmartre, 18th arr. · Free (basilica); dome climb 8€ · ★ 4.7 (164,005 Google reviews)
📍 35 Rue du Chevalier de la Barre, 75018 Paris (48.8867, 2.3431)
> Gleaming white basilica crowning Montmartre with one of the best free panoramas of Paris from its steps. Sunset is magic but crowded — watch for pickpockets and bracelet scammers on the stairs.
🗣️ Google reviewers: Free entry, glittering mosaics, and panoramic city views; complaints about heavy crowds, the steep climb, and pushy vendors outside.
💡 *Climb the 300-step dome before 10am for a crowd-free view; take the Anvers funicular up (one Metro ticket).*

### Arc de Triomphe
**Monument** · 8th arr. · 22€ non-EEA / 16€ EEA adults, free under 18 · ★ 4.7 (230,000 Google reviews)
📍 Place Charles de Gaulle, 75008 Paris (48.8738, 2.295)
> Napoleon's triumphal arch with a rooftop terrace many rate the best viewpoint in Paris — it includes the Eiffel Tower and the 12 radiating avenues. 284-step spiral climb; midday queues.
🗣️ Google reviewers: The rooftop panorama — especially at night when the Eiffel Tower sparkles — is worth it; gripes about the 284-step climb and confusion over the underground access tunnel.
💡 *Go after dark (open until ~10:30pm) for the illuminated Champs-Élysées; always use the underground passage, never cross the roundabout.*

### Musée d'Orsay
**Museum** · 7th arr. · 16€ online / 14€ on-site, 12€ Thursday evenings, free under 18 · ★ 4.8 (115,000 Google reviews)
📍 Esplanade Valéry Giscard d'Estaing, 75007 Paris (48.86, 2.3266)
> The world's greatest Impressionist collection (Monet, Van Gogh, Renoir) in a spectacular Beaux-Arts train station. More manageable and enjoyable than the Louvre; 5th-floor galleries pack out by midday.
🗣️ Google reviewers: World-class Impressionist collection in a stunning former railway station; main complaint is crowds and waits at the most famous rooms.
💡 *Thursday after 6pm is cheaper and calmer; head straight to the 5th floor for the famous clock-window photo.*

### Sainte-Chapelle
**Church** · Île de la Cité, 1st arr. · 22€ non-EEA / 16€ EEA; combined with Conciergerie 30€/23€ · ★ 4.6 (49,079 Google reviews)
📍 10 Boulevard du Palais, 75001 Paris (48.8554, 2.345)
> A 13th-century Gothic jewel box with 15-metre stained-glass walls routinely called the most beautiful interior in Paris. Small, so security lines can exceed the visit time without a timed ticket.
🗣️ Google reviewers: The 15 floor-to-ceiling stained-glass windows are called the most beautiful in Europe and photos don't do them justice; frequent complaints about chaotic entry lines even with timed tickets.
💡 *Book the first slot on a sunny morning or late afternoon when light blazes through the glass.*

### Palais Garnier (Opéra Garnier)
**Monument** · 9th arr. · 15€ self-guided visit, free under 12 · ★ 4.7 (55,000 Google reviews)
📍 Place de l'Opéra, 75009 Paris (48.872, 2.3316)
> Opulent 19th-century opera house famed for its marble Grand Staircase, gilded Grand Foyer and Chagall ceiling — the Phantom of the Opera setting. Auditorium can be closed for rehearsals on visit days.
🗣️ Google reviewers: Reviewers gush over the opulent grand staircase, gilded interiors, and Chagall ceiling; occasional complaints that the auditorium is closed for rehearsals without warning.
💡 *Check the rehearsal calendar before booking; or buy a cheap seat to an actual ballet instead of the day visit.*

### Seine River Cruise (Bateaux Mouches)
**Boat Tour** · 8th arr. · 18€ adults (17€ online), 8€ children · ★ 4.4 (35,000 Google reviews)
📍 Port de la Conférence, Pont de l'Alma, 75008 Paris (48.8639, 2.3055)
> The classic one-hour loop past Notre-Dame, the Louvre and the Eiffel Tower — the easiest way to see the monuments. Daytime boats can feel crowded.
🗣️ Google reviewers: Monument views from the water and good value, with dinner cruises praised for food and live music; complaints target the loud multilingual audio commentary and slow boarding.
💡 *Take the departure just before sunset: golden-hour light plus the Eiffel Tower's on-the-hour sparkle from the water.*

### Palace of Versailles
**Day Trip** · Versailles (day trip) · Passport 35€ high / 25€ low season (non-EEA); gardens free most days · ★ 4.6 (250,000 Google reviews)
📍 Place d'Armes, 78000 Versailles (48.8049, 2.1204)
> Louis XIV's staggering palace, Hall of Mirrors and vast gardens — the top day trip from Paris (RER C, ~45 min). Unmissable but extremely crowded by late morning; timed entry mandatory.
🗣️ Google reviewers: The Hall of Mirrors and gardens are jaw-dropping and worth a full day; the dominant complaint is extreme crowds and long queues even with advance tickets.
💡 *Reverse the crowds: start with the gardens and Trianon, then enter the palace after 3pm when tour groups leave. Avoid Tuesdays and weekends.*

### Catacombs of Paris
**Monument** · Denfert-Rochereau, 14th arr. · 31€ adults online with audio guide, 12€ ages 5-17 · ★ 4.1 (10,500 Google reviews)
📍 1 Avenue du Colonel Henri Rol-Tanguy, 75014 Paris (48.8339, 2.3324)
> A 1.5 km walk through tunnels lined with the bones of six million Parisians, 20 metres underground — fascinating and genuinely eerie. On-site tickets nearly impossible; 131 steps down, 112 up, no lift.
🗣️ Google reviewers: The bone-lined tunnels are an eerie, unforgettable experience with a good audio guide; complaints about tickets selling out, the steep price, and the demanding stairs.
💡 *Online slots release 7 days ahead and vanish fast — book at the exact release time. Bring a jacket; it's a constant 14°C.*

### Centre Pompidou (exterior)
**Museum** · Beaubourg, 4th arr. · Closed for renovation until 2030; exterior and piazza free · ★ 4.4 (57,000 Google reviews)
📍 Place Georges-Pompidou, 75004 Paris (48.8607, 2.3522)
> The inside-out high-tech landmark is closed for full renovation since September 2025 (reopens 2030), but the building and lively Beaubourg piazza are still worth a look in passing.
🗣️ Google reviewers: The modern art collection and rooftop views are praised; recent frustration centers on the museum's closure for multi-year renovation.
💡 *Collection highlights are touring partner venues via the 'Constellation' programme, including the Grand Palais.*

### Jardin du Luxembourg
**Park** · 6th arr. · Free · ★ 4.7 (109,873 Google reviews)
📍 Rue de Médicis / Rue de Vaugirard, 75006 Paris (48.8462, 2.3372)
> The Left Bank's beloved 25-hectare garden with the octagonal pond, Medici Fountain and famous green chairs — the perfect break between Saint-Germain and the Latin Quarter. Closes at dusk.
🗣️ Google reviewers: Immaculate lawns, fountains, and iconic green chairs make a peaceful escape; weekend overcrowding is the main complaint.
💡 *Grab a low reclining green chair by the pond; the shaded Medici Fountain in the northeast corner is the spot most tourists never find.*

### Montmartre & Place du Tertre
**Neighborhood Walk** · Montmartre, 18th arr. · Free · ★ 4.5 (17,000 Google reviews)
📍 Place du Tertre, 75018 Paris (48.8865, 2.3407)
> The hilltop artists' village of cobbled lanes and portrait painters, with vineyard-and-windmill corners. Adored atmosphere, but midday crowds and tourist-trap cafés on the square are intense.
🗣️ Google reviewers: The charming cobbled square full of working portrait artists is enjoyed, but reviewers repeatedly note it is extremely touristy with overpriced restaurants.
💡 *Explore before 9am or at dusk via the back streets — Rue de l'Abreuvoir, the Maison Rose and Place Dalida are prettier and emptier.*

### Champs-Élysées
**Avenue** · 8th arr. · Free · ★ 4.7 (95,000 Google reviews)
📍 Avenue des Champs-Élysées, 75008 Paris (48.8698, 2.3076)
> The 1.9 km 'most beautiful avenue in the world' from Concorde to the Arc de Triomphe, lined with flagships and cafés. A fun stroll but commercial and pickpocket-prone.
🗣️ Google reviewers: The stroll from the Arc de Triomphe toward Concorde is iconic, especially lit up at night; it feels commercialized and crowded with real pickpocket risk.
💡 *Walk it on the first Sunday of the month when it goes car-free; detour into the quieter gardens at the Concorde end.*

### Panthéon
**Monument** · Latin Quarter, 5th arr. · 16€ non-EEA / 13€ EEA; dome supplement ~3.50€ (Apr-Oct) · ★ 4.6 (47,000 Google reviews)
📍 Place du Panthéon, 75005 Paris (48.8462, 2.3464)
> Neoclassical mausoleum of France's greats — Voltaire, Hugo, Curie — with Foucault's pendulum under the dome. Uncrowded compared with other landmarks.
🗣️ Google reviewers: The massive dome, Foucault's pendulum, and the moving crypt of Voltaire and Marie Curie are praised, with only mild complaints about queues and limited dome-access slots.
💡 *April-October, buy the dome access supplement on arrival — its 360° colonnade view is one of Paris's least-known panoramas.*

### Trocadéro Gardens & Esplanade
**Viewpoint** · 16th arr. · Free · ★ 4.6 (150,000 Google reviews)
📍 Place du Trocadéro-et-du-11-Novembre, 75016 Paris (48.8616, 2.2893)
> The terrace between the Palais de Chaillot wings delivers the most famous head-on view of the Eiffel Tower across the Seine fountains. Essential photo stop; dawn-to-dusk crowds, vendors and pickpockets.
🗣️ Google reviewers: The single best photo spot for the Eiffel Tower with lively fountains; warnings about sunset crowds, aggressive street vendors, and pickpockets.
💡 *Come at sunrise for an almost-empty esplanade and pink light, or the lower garden lawns for the same view with a fraction of the people.*

### Conciergerie
**Monument** · Île de la Cité, 1st arr. · Combined with Sainte-Chapelle 30€ non-EEA / 23€ EEA · ★ 4.4 (12,000 Google reviews)
📍 2 Boulevard du Palais, 75001 Paris (48.8561, 2.3454)
> Medieval royal palace turned Revolutionary prison where Marie-Antoinette awaited the guillotine. Vast Gothic hall and immersive HistoPad tablets; far less crowded than Sainte-Chapelle next door.
🗣️ Google reviewers: The French Revolution history and Marie Antoinette's cell are fascinating, especially with the HistoPad AR tablet; some feel the largely empty halls need that context.
💡 *Buy the combined ticket and do the Conciergerie first — its line is much shorter and the timed Sainte-Chapelle slot becomes your skip-ahead.*


## 🌿 Local Favorites (16)
*Where Parisians actually go*

### Canal Saint-Martin

### Pont des Arts
**Viewpoint** · Saint-Germain/Louvre, 1st-6th arr. · Free · ★ 4.6 (20,000 Google reviews)
📍 Pont des Arts, between Quai François Mitterrand and Quai de Conti, 75006 Paris (48.8584, 2.3375)
> Wooden-decked pedestrian bridge with the Institut de France dome on one side and the Louvre on the other, the Seine threading toward Île de la Cité. Picnickers and buskers at sunset make great candid material.
🗣️ Google reviewers: Reviewers call it the most romantic sunset spot on the Seine with painters and picnickers adding atmosphere.
💡 *At golden hour face east toward Pont Neuf for backlit river haze; the Institut dome shot works best in morning light.*

### Statue of Liberty, Île aux Cygnes
**Photo Spot** · Javel/Grenelle, 15th arr. · Free · ★ 4.5 (6,000 Google reviews)
📍 Southwestern tip of Île aux Cygnes, at Pont de Grenelle, 75015 Paris (48.85, 2.2797)
> Quarter-scale 1889 Statue of Liberty replica facing her New York sister, reached by a peaceful tree-lined island walkway with the Eiffel Tower upstream. One of the calmest photo spots on the Seine.
🗣️ Google reviewers: Reviewers enjoy the surreal New-York-in-Paris shot and the quiet riverside promenade.
💡 *From the Pont de Grenelle sidewalk you can align Liberty's torch with the Eiffel Tower behind her — best in late afternoon western sun.*

### Parc de Saint-Cloud — La Lanterne Overlook
**Viewpoint** · Saint-Cloud, west of Paris · Free for pedestrians · ★ 4.6 (20,000 Google reviews)
📍 Rond de la Balustrade, Domaine national de Saint-Cloud, 92210 Saint-Cloud (48.8366, 2.2151)
> Hilltop belvedere at 162m in a former royal park, with a wide panorama over the Seine and the whole Paris skyline from La Défense to the Eiffel Tower. Spacious and uncrowded even on weekends — a local picnic-and-sunset spot.
🗣️ Google reviewers: Park reviewers single out the Lanterne terrace as a breathtaking, little-known panorama of all Paris.
💡 *Come 30-45 minutes before sunset with a zoom lens to compress the Eiffel Tower against the city; mornings give clearer air after rain.*

### Hôtel de Ville Carousel
**Photo Spot** · Hôtel-de-Ville, 4th arr. · Free to photograph (ride ~4€) · ★ 4.5 (1,000 Google reviews)
📍 Place de l'Hôtel de Ville, 75004 Paris (48.8572, 2.3508)
> Belle Époque double-decker carousel spinning in front of the neo-Renaissance city hall facade — a storybook Paris frame, especially when both are lit after dark.
🗣️ Google reviewers: Reviewers say the illuminated carousel against the Hôtel de Ville is one of the city's prettiest evening scenes.
💡 *Shoot at blue hour with a quarter-second exposure so the spinning carousel blurs while the floodlit facade stays sharp.*

### Sinking House of Montmartre
**Photo Spot** · Montmartre, 18th arr. · Free · ★ 4.3 (300 Google reviews)
📍 Grass slope east of the Sacré-Cœur steps, Square Louise Michel, 75018 Paris (48.8862, 2.3433)
> Famous optical-illusion shot: tilt your camera along the steep lawn beside Sacré-Cœur and the building behind appears to sink into a level hillside. The lawn area is far quieter than the main steps.
🗣️ Google reviewers: Visitors call it a quirky must-try illusion, though many walk past without realizing where to stand.
💡 *Stand on the grass slope east of the funicular, rotate the camera ~45° until the lawn looks horizontal, and crop out the sky edges.*

### Passerelle Debilly
**Viewpoint** · Trocadéro/Gros-Caillou, 7th-16th arr. · Free · ★ 4.6 (3,000 Google reviews)
📍 Passerelle Debilly, opposite Palais de Tokyo, 75007/75116 Paris (48.863, 2.2944)
> Quiet 1900 iron footbridge with an oblique, close-range view of the Eiffel Tower over the Seine — the favored alternative when Bir-Hakeim is mobbed. Almost empty at dawn.
🗣️ Google reviewers: Reviewers describe it as an underrated, peaceful spot for hourly sparkle shows and river-level tower shots.
💡 *Stand mid-bridge on the upstream rail at dusk: the tower's sparkle reflects in the Seine, and boat light trails work well with a 10-second exposure.*
